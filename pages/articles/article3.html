Product Review: All baskets were not created equal

When it comes to bicycle baskets, not all are created equal. Some prioritize style, while others emphasize durability and capacity. Wicker baskets provide a vintage aesthetic but can struggle under heavy loads, while metal wire baskets offer sturdiness but may not be as visually appealing. Modern designs, such as quick-release baskets, allow for greater versatility, enabling cyclists to detach and carry them like shopping bags.

With growing urban cycling trends, selecting the right basket depends on individual needs—whether it’s for commuting, grocery shopping, or simply enhancing the bike’s look. The key is to find a balance between functionality and design.

Going Public: Out & about with the founder of Public

Public Bikes has revolutionized urban cycling, making stylish and functional city bikes more accessible. The company's founder, Rob Forbes, envisioned a world where people preferred two wheels over four, creating bicycles that are both practical and elegant. Public’s Dutch-style bikes prioritize comfort with upright riding positions, wide tires, and simple gear mechanisms.

As more cities invest in bike-friendly infrastructure, the demand for these urban-focused bicycles has grown. Forbes continues to advocate for sustainable mobility, believing that well-designed bicycles can influence lifestyle changes, encouraging people to embrace cycling for daily commutes and leisure.

Cycle Lane Defence: Know your rights

Cyclists often face challenges when navigating urban streets, including reckless drivers, unclear traffic laws, and a lack of dedicated cycling infrastructure. However, understanding legal rights can help ensure a safer ride. In many cities, cyclists are entitled to the full lane when necessary for safety, and laws require vehicles to maintain a minimum passing distance.
